In grade 9, I took a course that allowed me to experiment with Photoshop for the first time. I immediately became intruiged with it's features creating all sorts of funny posters, interesting graphics and conceptual art. I decided that it was something I enjoyed doing so I continued experimenting even after the course had finished. I began finding new ways to improve my skills, first by learning from experienced creators on the internet but eventually joining organizations with like-minded people who taught me some to the coolest things I know today. I learned that whether I was a graphic designer or video editor or illustrator, it wasn't just about the content I produced, but also my ability to work with and understand others, even if it means having to go through the design process numerous times until it becomes perfect. Of course, I'm a long way from perfect and I'm constantly trying to find new ways to improve my skills, whether that would be joining new organizations, or meeting new and interesting people, which is why I created this website to document and show my journey to myself and anyone who may be interested.
